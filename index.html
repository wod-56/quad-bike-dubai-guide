Safety Rules Tourists Must Follow

  • Always wear a helmet and protective gear
  • Follow the guide’s speed and distance instructions
  • Avoid sharp turns on steep dunes
  • Maintain a safe gap between riders
  • Do not attempt stunts or racing
  • Stay hydrated and take breaks when needed

What to Wear for a Safe Ride

  • Closed-toe shoes (no sandals)
  • Comfortable long trousers
  • Lightweight long-sleeve shirt
  • Sunglasses or goggles
  • Sunscreen for daytime rides

Best Time for a Safer Experience

  • Morning: cooler temperatures and softer sand
  • Sunset: better visibility and comfortable weather
  • Avoid midday rides in summer due to heat

Health and Fitness Considerations

Quad biking does not require advanced fitness, but riders should have basic mobility and be comfortable handling handlebars for extended periods. People with serious back, neck, or heart conditions should consult a medical professional before participating.

Tips for First-Time Riders

  • Start with a smaller engine quad bike
  • Listen carefully during the safety briefing
  • Keep both hands on the handlebars
  • Lean slightly forward when climbing dunes
  • Brake gently while descending

Environmental and Responsible Riding

Responsible operators emphasize eco-friendly riding by sticking to marked trails and avoiding wildlife zones. This helps preserve the desert landscape while keeping riders safe.
